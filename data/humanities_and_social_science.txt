# Humanities and Social Science Overview

## History

History studies past events, societies, institutions, and ideas. Historians use sources such as documents, inscriptions, artifacts, and oral accounts to interpret change over time.

Historical explanation often focuses on causes, consequences, continuity, and turning points. For example, industrialization changed production, labor, cities, and global trade patterns.

## Economics

Economics studies how individuals and societies allocate scarce resources. Microeconomics focuses on households, firms, prices, and markets. Macroeconomics studies national income, inflation, unemployment, growth, and policy.

Supply and demand explain how prices coordinate decisions in markets. Opportunity cost, incentives, elasticity, and comparative advantage are foundational ideas.

## Political Science and Civics

Political science examines power, institutions, governance, law, and public policy. Civics focuses on the rights and duties of citizens and the functioning of government.

Common topics include constitutions, elections, legislatures, separation of powers, public administration, and international relations.

## Literature

Literature studies written works such as poetry, drama, fiction, and essays. Literary analysis considers theme, character, setting, plot, language, symbol, tone, and historical context.

Interpretation often asks how a text creates meaning and how it reflects cultural or philosophical concerns.

## Philosophy

Philosophy examines fundamental questions about knowledge, reality, ethics, logic, and the mind. Major branches include metaphysics, epistemology, ethics, political philosophy, and aesthetics.

Philosophical reasoning emphasizes clear definitions, careful argument, and critical examination of assumptions.
